Marks for Style

January 4, 2018 in meta, R, R Studio, Timeline

Blogging is old hat to me, but data science is brand new. I’m studying this subject (and a survey of all its componenet parts) like Homer here. This nervous energy is why my first few posts are all about what I do know: Juvenile justice, JEMS, the court, and blogging. Most of these early posts are going to address these topics because it will help establish context. Blogging about what I know does not mean that I’m not honing my R (and github) skill set..

The blog is written in R Studio with Yihui Xie’s Blogdown package. It’s hosted thanks to a combination of github, Hugo, and netlify. While the blogdown book has been my goto resource for putting this together, I’ve also read (and highly recommend):

  • Amber Thomas
  • Alison Presmanes Hill

The work of those three people is clear, concise, fun, and inspirational.

I am going to try and put up one visualization each week, and use the weekend to delve deeper into my education in stats, R programming, and machine learning. I will also post about my progress as I narrow down the questions I want to answer, and get a clearer picture about how I’ll use a neural network1 to solve it. If I a lucky, I will also get a few interviews. What’s the point in posting all of this if no converations come from it?

Speaking of conversations, now that disqus is working, feel free to leave a comment! I’m looking for all sorts of feedback on this project!

  1. When I started this project, I briefly considred a classification problem that would probably have benefited from a neural network. In the weeks of research that followed, I decided that it may not be the best fit afterall. But that’s a post for next week.↩
